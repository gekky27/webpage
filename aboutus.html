Our History and Story

Since its founding in early 2012 by Debby Lukito Goeyardi and Dewa Gede Agung Dharmayasa, Kanaditya has carried out regular activities in various locations, reaching diverse communities. These activities take the form of mobile programs, where Kanaditya takes a proactive approach—bringing the programs directly to the people. The goal is to reach as many children and communities as possible, especially those from marginalized backgrounds, who often face economic challenges that limit their ability to travel far from home.
Kanaditya’s core mission is to empower children and communities through skill-building based on the six foundational literacies. Over time, Kanaditya has expanded its training to include Culinary Literacy and Ecological Literacy—both of which have proven especially relevant during the ongoing pandemic.
Kanaditya believes that by equipping its beneficiaries with life skills, they can achieve financial independence through Financial Literacy, and adopt healthier, more sustainable lifestyles rooted in green thinking. This vision aims to shape a resilient generation—one that is food secure, literate, curious, appreciative of local culture, and guided by strong moral values drawn from Indonesia’s rich local wisdom.

Guided by the Sun: The Philosophy Behind Kanaditya

The name Kanaditya is a combination of two Sanskrit words: Kanaka and Aditya, both of which mean Sun. The philosophy of the Sun forms the foundation of all activities carried out by Kanaditya. The Sun shines selflessly, never breaks its promises, and never discriminates in sharing its light. Like the Sun, Kanaditya strives to radiate its light in the form of kindness to those in need, especially children, women, and marginalized communities. Kanaditya hopes that anyone who receives help from Kanaditya can, in turn, become a light for themselves and those around them.

In addition to empowering the community, Kanaditya also
focuses on activities that align with the Foundation’s Articles of Association and Bylaws, as follows:
  1. Melakukan kegiatan di bidang literasi (mencakup 6 literasi dasar), pendidikan, sosial dan kemanusiaan.
  2. Meningkatkan atau membangun Sumber Daya Manusia (SDM) dan fasilitas pendidikan serta perpustakaan lokal (formal/non-formal) dan kemasyarakatan demi tercapainya peningkatan kualitas pendidikan dan indeks pembangunan manusia.
  3. Memberikan bantuan dan meningkatkan kesejahteraan masyarakat.
  4. Melakukan kerjasama dengan badan-badan lain yang mempunyai maksud dan tujuan yang sama dengan Yayasan baik dari dalam negeri maupun luar negeri.
  1. Mendirikan lembaga pendidikan formal dan non formal serta pelatihan.
  2. Mendirikan rumah singgah, klinik kesehatan dan rumah sakit di wilayah Negara Republik Indonesia.
  3. Memberikan bantuan kepada korban bencana alam, pengungsi akibat perang, tuna wisma, fakir miskin dan gelandangan.
  4. Melestarikan lingkungan hidup.
  5. Membimbing dan menumbuhkan masyarakat berbasis sociopreneur.
